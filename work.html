Tracking Storms by Forerunners of Swell

The Southern Ocean has the stongest surface winds on the planet. Estimating their position from sattelites is a hard problem. Here is a video that illustrates how seismic observations in Antarctica can be used to to verify the position of high wind speeds and extra-tropical cyclones over the Southern Ocean using swell waves. This work is inspired by tracking storms by forerunners of swell form Walter Munk (1946).
