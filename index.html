About

I’m a third year graduate student at Scripps Institute of Oceanography and interested in climate variability, air-sea-interaction, high latitudes, oceans, jazz, philosophy.

My Research interest includes climate variability on timescales longer then todays weather predictions are skillfull, Southern Ocean winds and sea ice. I like to combine oceanographic, meteorological and cryosphere data as well as idealized models to extend our knowledge of climate dynamics of the coupled atmosphere-ocean system. I use Geophysical Fluid Dynamics, non-linear optimization methods and Models with the over all goal to reduce complexity and gain intuitive understanding of the earth system.

I am involved in the Ice Shelf Vibration Project on the Ross Ice Shelf on the Ross Ice Shelf and spend some time of the Ice Shelf in Antarctica. This data is used to understand southern ocean wind and waves and there impact Ice Shelfs, which is connected to sea level rise .

I studied at GEOMAR in Kiel (2009-2012) and ETH Zurich (2013-2015) Atmosphere, Ocean and Climate and spend several months at GFI Bergen, Norway, as visiting student between 2012 and 2018 (CV).

I have a background in graphic design and typography and implement these rules in scientific graphics and visualization in (science journals ).
