About

I’m a first year graduate student at Scripps Institute of Oceanography and interested in climate variability, air-sea-interaction, the arctic, oceans, jazz, philosophy and ways to a sustainable world. I grown up in northern Germany spending the summer on a boat sailing across the baltic sea. It turned out, that this guided my education and research more then I thought.

My Research interests are climate variability on timescales longterm then todays weather prediction systems can acheaf. Especially the role of the artic in the coupled climate system on sub seasonal to decadal scales is be in focus of my research. Air-sea-ice interaction as also momentum and heat transfer between ocean and atmosphere are key processes.

I studied at GEOMAR, Kiel (2009-2012) and ETH Zurich (2013-2015) Atmosphere, Ocean and Climate and spend several months at GFI Bergen, Norway, as visiting student between 2012 and 2015. Further informations about my research interests and CV. Beside the physics I took the chance to take course in science-related philosophy.

I’m family-biased to graphic design, typography and aesthetic rules of publishing. I try to implement these rules on the area of scientific graphics and visualisation and these pages.
