About

I am a graduate student at the Scripps Institute of Oceanography with Arthur "Art" Miller, Sarah Gille, and Bruce Cornuelle. My interested spans from climate variability and large-scale dynamics to air-sea-interaction and mixed-layer dynamics to surface waves and sea ice.

My research focuses on the Southern Ocean surface and air-sea interaction on many scales. For example, I work on swell wave generation under extra-tropical storm and how they can be used to understand mixed-layers. But I also work on surface wind statistics, stratospheric vortex dynamics, wave-ice interaction, and atmospheric blocking. I combine concepts from oceanography, meteorological, and geophysics and use Geophysical Fluid Dynamics, non-linear optimization, and idealized models to gain insight into processes at the atmosphere-ocean interface.

I was involved in the Ice Shelf Vibration Project on the Ross Ice Shelf and spent some time in Antarctica. This data was used to understand Southern Ocean wind and waves, and there impact Ice Shelves.

I have a graphic design and typography background and implement these rules for scientific graphics and visualization in science journals.

I studied at GEOMAR in Kiel (2009-2012) and ETH Zurich (2013-2015) Atmosphere, Ocean, and Climate. I spent several months at GFI Bergen, Norway, as a visiting student between 2012 and 2018 (CV).
